After retiring as a player, Amani took a deliberate path into management. He spent several years studying coaching methods, working as a technical advisor, and assisting at youth levels before taking charge of Real Castellón’s B team in 2015. In 2017, he was appointed head coach of the senior squad, leading them to extraordinary success: three consecutive Champions League titles and a league campaign often described as one of the most tactically innovative in modern Spanish football. His coaching style emphasized player psychology, spatial control, and fluid attacking structures, proving that his brilliance was not limited to the pitch.
